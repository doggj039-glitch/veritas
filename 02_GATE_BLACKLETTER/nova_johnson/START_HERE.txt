========================================================================
  START HERE  --  Meet NOVA JOHNSON
========================================================================

WHAT THIS IS
------------
NOVA JOHNSON is your founding-era definition agent. You give him a word,
and he gives you the plain meaning of that word from your own sources.

The key thing: he cannot make up a definition. He only reads what is
actually in your Definitions Library. If a word is not there, he says
"No verified definition found in loaded sources" and stops. That is on
purpose -- it is the whole reason he exists.

Nothing in here touches VERITAS. It is a safe, self-contained test, just
like the gate packet. Open it, try it, close it -- nothing else changes.


WHAT IS INSIDE
--------------
  1. START_HERE.txt ............... this guide.
  2. nova_johnson.py .............. the agent you will start. The one you touch.
  3. NOVA_JOHNSON_ROLE_CARD.txt ... his written rules and character (read anytime).
  4. veritas_libraries.py ......... the gate he reads through.
  5. VERITAS_definitions_library.json .. your plain definitions.
  6. etymology_dictionary.py ...... your word origins (support only).
  7. VERITAS_historical_context_library.json .. your historical notes.

All seven must stay together in the same folder. That is why they are
packed together here.


THE STEPS
---------

STEP 1 -- Save the packet.
  Click the packet file NOVA_JOHNSON_AGENT.zip so it saves onto your
  computer. It lands in your Downloads area (where saved things go).

STEP 2 -- Find it.
  Open your file manager (the program showing your folders -- the small
  filing-cabinet icon). Click "Downloads" on the left. You will see
  NOVA_JOHNSON_AGENT.zip.

STEP 3 -- Unpack it.
  Right-click NOVA_JOHNSON_AGENT.zip one time. Click "Extract Here."
  WHY: the packet arrives squeezed into one file so nothing gets
  separated. "Extract" un-squeezes it into a normal folder.
  You will see a new folder named NOVA_JOHNSON_AGENT.

STEP 4 -- Open the new folder.
  Double-click the new folder NOVA_JOHNSON_AGENT to open it. You should
  see the seven items listed above.

STEP 5 -- Open a typing window inside that folder.
  Right-click an empty space inside this folder (on the blank area, not
  on a file). Click "Open Terminal Here."
  WHY: a "terminal" is just a small window where you type one instruction
  to the computer. Opening it inside this folder means the computer
  already knows where the agent's files are.

STEP 6 -- Start the agent.
  Type this line exactly, then press Enter:

        python3 nova_johnson.py

  WHY, in plain terms:
    - "python3" is a program already on your computer that knows how to
      read this kind of file.
    - "nova_johnson.py" is the agent file you want it to read.
    - Typing them with one space between, then pressing Enter, tells the
      computer: "use Python to read the nova_johnson file now." That
      starts NOVA JOHNSON.

STEP 7 -- Talk to him.
  He first shows three examples -- including "equity," which he REFUSES,
  because it is not in your library. Then he says "Now you try." Type any
  word and press Enter to see his answer. Type the word  done  to finish.


WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE
-----------------------
  - Words that ARE in your library (liberty, shall, commerce) come back
    with a real founding-era definition.
  - Words that are NOT (like equity) come back with:
        "No verified definition found in loaded sources."
  If you see him answering the real ones and refusing the missing ones,
  he works. That refusal is the feature, not a bug.


IF SOMETHING LOOKS WRONG
------------------------
  - If Step 6 says "command not found," the Python program is not on this
    computer yet. Stop and tell me; I will walk you through adding it.
  - If it says it cannot find a file, the seven items got separated. Put
    them back in one folder and try Step 5 again from inside it.
  - Anything else: copy what the window says, tell me, and I will fix it.


A NOTE ON WHICH COMPUTER
------------------------
These steps are written for your Linux computer. On the Windows machine
the only change is Step 5 and the exact word in Step 6 ("python" instead
of "python3"). Tell me if you are on Windows and I will hand you that
version.
