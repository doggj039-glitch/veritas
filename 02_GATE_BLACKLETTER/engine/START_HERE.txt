  START HERE  --  Run the Blackletter Engine
========================================================================

WHAT THIS IS
------------
This is the engine -- the rule-runner at the heart of Blackletter. You
give it a case, it gives you back one plain answer and shows every step
it took to get there.

This packet runs five example cases for you, one for each kind of answer
the engine can give. Nothing here touches VERITAS. It is safe and self-
contained. Open it, watch it, close it -- nothing else changes.


WHAT IS INSIDE
--------------
  1. START_HERE.txt ................. this guide.
  2. RUN_THE_ENGINE.py .............. the demonstration you will start.
  3. ENGINE_HOW_IT_WORKS.txt ........ the doctrine, and how to continue it.
  4. blackletter_engine.py .......... the engine itself.
  5. rules (a folder) ............... the rules, kept as data:
        speech-protection.json
        enumerated-authority.json
  6. veritas_libraries.py ........... the gate the engine resolves words through.
  7. VERITAS_definitions_library.json .. your definitions.
  8. etymology_dictionary.py ........ your word origins.
  9. VERITAS_historical_context_library.json .. your historical notes.

Everything must stay together in one folder.


THE STEPS
---------

STEP 1 -- Save the packet.
  Click the packet file BLACKLETTER_ENGINE.zip so it saves onto your
  computer. It lands in your Downloads area.

STEP 2 -- Find it.
  Open your file manager (the small filing-cabinet icon that shows your
  folders). Click "Downloads" on the left. You will see
  BLACKLETTER_ENGINE.zip.

STEP 3 -- Unpack it.
  Right-click BLACKLETTER_ENGINE.zip one time. Click "Extract Here."
  A new folder named BLACKLETTER_ENGINE appears.

STEP 4 -- Open the new folder.
  Double-click the new folder BLACKLETTER_ENGINE to open it. You should
  see the items listed above, including the 'rules' folder.

STEP 5 -- Open a typing window inside that folder.
  Right-click an empty space inside this folder (on the blank area, not
  on a file). Click "Open Terminal Here." A small window opens with a
  blinking cursor.

STEP 6 -- Start the engine.
  Type this line exactly, then press Enter:

        python3 RUN_THE_ENGINE.py

  In plain terms: "python3" is a program already on your computer that
  reads this kind of file; "RUN_THE_ENGINE.py" is the file you want it to
  read. Typing them with a space between and pressing Enter starts it.

STEP 7 -- Read what it prints.
  It runs five cases and shows, for each one, the answer in big letters
  and every step it took to reach it.


WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE
-----------------------
You will see five different answers, each with its trace:

  ABRIDGMENT        -- a permit-and-fee on political pamphlets
  VALID             -- a town repaving a street (no right touched)
  VOID FOR VAGUENESS-- a case leaning on the word "privacy," which is not
                       in your library, so the engine stops on that word
  RESERVED          -- a claimed federal power that is not enumerated
  ULTRA VIRES       -- an army appropriation for five years, crossing the
                       written two-year limit

If you see five different answers, each with a trace showing how it got
there, the engine works. When you are ready to build on it, open
ENGINE_HOW_IT_WORKS.txt -- it explains how to add your own rules and cases.


IF SOMETHING LOOKS WRONG
------------------------
  - If Step 6 says "command not found," Python is not on this computer
    yet. Stop and tell me; I will walk you through adding it.
  - If it cannot find a file, the items got separated. Put them all back
    in one folder (with the 'rules' folder inside it) and try Step 5 again.
  - Anything else: copy what the window says and tell me.


A NOTE ON WHICH COMPUTER
------------------------
Written for your Linux computer. On Windows, only Step 5 and the exact
word in Step 6 change ("python" instead of "python3"). Tell me if you are
on Windows and I will hand you that version.
