# VERITAS — An Introduction

## The idea in one breath

The Constitution was written by specific people, at a specific time, in a specific language, using a
specific set of rules for how legal writing was to be read. VERITAS puts those two things — **the
words as they were defined then, and the rules for reading them as they were used then** — back
together, so anyone can see what a passage meant on the day it was written, and measure how far the
meaning has drifted since.

It does not tell you what the law *should* be. It shows you, from the record, what the words *meant* —
and lets you ask the question yourself.

---

## Why it stands on two pillars, not one

Most arguments about the Constitution reach for a modern dictionary or a modern assumption. VERITAS
refuses both, because in 1787 a legal document was read two ways at once:

### Pillar 1 — What the words meant: **Johnson's Dictionary (1755 / 1773)**
When the founders wrote "commerce," "person," "liberty," "religion," they were using words with
settled, published meanings — and the authority of the day was **Samuel Johnson's *Dictionary of the
English Language***. The 1773 edition is the one closest to the 1787 framing. VERITAS stores each
word's Johnson definition **verbatim** — never paraphrased, always with its provenance — as the
baseline meaning. That is the anchor: not what "person" means to us, but what it meant to a literate
reader in the founding era.

### Pillar 2 — How the words were read: **the rules of construction of the time**
A dictionary alone isn't enough. The founders were trained in a body of **interpretive rules** — the
same rules 18th-century lawyers used to read statutes and contracts — and those rules govern how the
words fit together. VERITAS applies them:

- **Expressio unius est exclusio alterius** — if the document lists specific things, whatever is *not*
  listed was left out on purpose. (The powers of Congress are enumerated; an unlisted power is excluded.)
- **Capitalized words are defined terms** — a Capitalized word signaled a fixed meaning drawn from
  common law and Johnson/Blackstone, not decoration.
- **Ejusdem generis** — a general word after a list of specifics is limited to the same category.
- **Noscitur a sociis** — a word is known by the words around it; context confirms or narrows meaning.
- **The rule against surplusage** — every word carries meaning; if two different words appear, they
  mean two different things; if a word is absent, that absence is deliberate.
- **Original public meaning** — a term means what it meant to an informed reader at the time of drafting;
  later re-definitions are *drift*, not the meaning.
- **The amendment process (Article V)** — the structure changes one way only; a structural change made
  by any other route bypassed the only valid mechanism.

**The point of the two pillars together:** a document read by its own era's dictionary *and* its own
era's rules of construction has a knowable meaning. VERITAS reconstructs that meaning from the record,
then — with its optional reasoning tier — measures, with cited modern sources, how far today's practice
has moved from it. Founding meaning is kept strictly separate from modern meaning, and both are kept
separate from any inference. Nothing is invented; everything is cited.

governing architecture is in `VERITAS_DESIGN.md`.)*

## An honest invitation

VERITAS was built by one person, largely solo, out of a conviction that the founding-era meaning of
our founding documents is knowable, checkable, and worth preserving — and that the drift away from it
is measurable rather than merely arguable.

It has reached the limit of what one self-taught builder can do alone. **It now needs people who are
far more capable — and it is being shared openly for exactly that reason.** If you are a
constitutional-law scholar, a legal historian, a linguist of period English, or an engineer who can
harden and extend it: **please take this further than its author could.**

- Pressure-test the interpretive method and the sourced analyses; correct what's wrong; make it represent
  competing readings fairly instead of picking a side.
- Improve the record, the provenance discipline, and the reasoning tier's soundness.
- Make the tool itself genuinely good — accurate, well-tested, easy to run and to trust.

This project is not anyone's possession to guard. The goal is a **trustworthy, checkable instrument**
for seeing what the words meant and how far we've moved — and to get there, it should be led by whoever
can make it best. **If that's you, this is an open door. See `NEXT_PHASE.md` and open an issue — even a
long, critical one. Disagreement is welcome; being corrected is the point.**

*VERITAS gathers the record — the era's words and the era's rules. The public sources make it checkable.
The reader asks the question. Help make every part of that better.*
