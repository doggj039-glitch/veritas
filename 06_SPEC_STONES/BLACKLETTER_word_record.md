# BLACKLETTER — THE WORD RECORD

*The shape of one word's full entry: every source stacked under one headword. The structure that makes duplication impossible.*

*Version 1 — captured before the records are built, per the Order of Operations (rule before action).*

---

## THE PROBLEM THIS SOLVES

The same word lived in many files, so it appeared many times — "act" shown over and over, each a fragment from a different source. That is not many words. It is one word scattered across many places.

The Word Record fixes this at the root: **one word becomes one record that holds all of its definitions across every source, stacked in time order.** Duplication becomes structurally impossible, because a word is a single object that gathers its sources — not many rows competing for attention.

---

## THE STRUCTURE

One record per word. Inside it, the sources are stacked in chronological order, oldest to newest, so the record reads as a drift timeline from the root forward.

**The record, top to bottom:**

1. **Root / Etymology** — language, root, literal meaning, earliest recorded date. The anchor.
2. **The source stack, in date order** — each source is one sense-entry:
   - 1721 — Bailey's Universal Etymological Dictionary
   - 1755 — Johnson's Dictionary
   - 1761 — Priestley, The Rudiments of English Grammar (usage/construction, not definition)
   - 1828 — Webster's American Dictionary
   - 1856 — Bouvier's Law Dictionary
   - 1889 — Century Dictionary
   - 1933 — Oxford English Dictionary (sense history by date)
   - 2004 — Black's Law Dictionary (8th)
   - current — Black's Law Dictionary (9th)
   - (plus the founding-era **usage** record — real quotes showing the word in use)
3. **Drift Summary** — the timeline collapsed to one line per era, showing the movement from root meaning to modern. Built from whatever columns are actually filled — never from placeholders.

Each sense-entry carries: its definition, its source, its date, its type (general / legal / grammar / historical), and any notes. (This matches the DictionaryEntry schema — each sense-entry IS one DictionaryEntry; the Word Record is the wrapper that holds many of them.)

---

## WHAT IS VISIBLE, WHAT IS HELD

This mirrors the gate's output order. The record holds everything; it does not show everything at once.

- **Visible by default — the three that matter:** Root / Etymology · Johnson 1755 · founding-era Usage. These are the two-to-three points of verification, shown up front.
- **Held behind the record — the drift timeline:** Bailey, Webster, Bouvier, Century, OED, Black's 8th and 9th. Present in the record, shown when the reader chooses to see how far the word has moved.

The record is the gate's answer shape and the drift archive in one object: the anchor and its verification forward, the full history behind.

---

## THE RULES FOR FILLING IT

These govern what is allowed into a record. They come straight from the Order of Operations.

### Only sourced data enters. Nothing is generated.
A column is filled only from a real, identified source. A plausible-sounding definition is never invented to fill a gap. The record can be sparse; it can never be fabricated.

### Empty is shown as empty.
A column with no verified source reads *"— not yet sourced,"* openly. A blank column must never look like a filled one. A record shows exactly how complete it is. This matters most here, because a full-looking drift table is the most persuasive place for fabrication to hide.

### Verified and unverified are marked, never blended.
Johnson entries pulled from the raw scan are marked *unverified — pending bench* until a human confirms them. A verified column and an unverified column are visibly different. The gate may treat only verified columns as controlling.

### Poisoned sources are not used to fill columns.
The corrupted definition databases are never a fill source, no matter how convenient. Columns fill from clean, identified sources only — or they stay honestly empty until a clean source exists.

### The drift summary is built from what is real.
The summary at the bottom is assembled only from columns that are actually filled and verified. It grows truthful as the record fills. It never shows a timeline built on placeholders or unverified guesses.

---

## HOW A RECORD GROWS

A record starts mostly empty and fills over time — honestly, at each step:

- **Now:** Etymology (curated), Johnson (raw, marked unverified), Usage (real quotes). The rest: not yet sourced.
- **As the bench runs:** Johnson columns move from unverified to verified, one word at a time.
- **As other sources are cleaned:** Bailey, Webster, and the rest light up, column by column, each from a clean source.
- **The drift summary deepens on its own** as real columns populate.

The structure is right and honest from the first day. Fullness comes later, earned column by column. A right-and-honest sparse record is worth more than a full-looking suspect one.

---

## THE ONE THING TO REMEMBER

One word, one record. Its definitions stacked across every source in time order. The first three visible, the rest held. Every column sourced or honestly empty — never invented, never blended, never filled from poison. The drift summary tells the truth because it is built only from what is real.
