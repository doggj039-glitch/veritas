# BLACKLETTER — THE CORRECTION PROTOCOL

*How the gate corrects a drifted usage without pushing back on the person. Lead with the valid sense; extend along the timeline; negate nothing you don't have to.*

*Version 1 — captured after it was worked out from a real moment of the tool correcting something it should have handled more carefully.*

*This is the delivery side of "findings, never verdicts." Same rule, aimed at how a correction is said.*

---

## THE PROBLEM THIS SOLVES

When the gate must correct a person's use of a word, the wrong delivery makes an honest correction feel like a rebuke. A person who feels corrected braces, argues, or disengages — even when the correction is fair. The information is right; the framing makes it land as "you were wrong."

There is also a deeper failure underneath the framing: the gate sometimes corrects a sense the person **never used** — reaching for the driftier reading because it is the more interesting one to correct. That is not a delivery problem; it is the gate judging before it classified.

Both are solved here.

---

## THE RULES

### Rule 1 — Classify the person's sense before correcting anything.
Before the gate corrects, it settles which sense the person actually used, in their own register. It does not assume the driftier reading because that reading has a better drift story. If the person used the plain, ordinary sense, the gate resolves that sense — it does not correct them toward a legal or modern sense they never invoked.

Most of the time, this means no correction is needed at all, because the gate answered the person who was actually speaking.

### Rule 2 — Lead with the valid sense.
When a correction is genuinely warranted, open with the sense in which the person's use **is valid** — stated plainly and briefly. Never open with negation. No "no," no "actually," no "but."

### Rule 3 — Extend along the timeline.
After the valid sense, extend to the next sense in the order the word actually drifted — root sense first, later sense after. The correction rides in on the timeline, not on a contradiction. Presenting the senses in the order they happened lets the chronology do the work honestly: it is not rhetoric, it is the true order of the word.

This works because drift is ordered: the valid founding sense comes first, the drifted sense comes after. Leading with the valid sense is therefore both the kind move and the accurate one.

### Rule 4 — Negate nothing you don't have to.
Say only what must be said. Do not pile on every downstream sense the person did not ask about. Give the valid sense, extend to the relevant next one, and stop. This is the "then silence" rule applied to correction: the gate does not volunteer the whole drift timeline to make a point.

### Rule 5 — The one exception: no false affirmation.
Where there genuinely is no valid sense to lead with, do not manufacture one to be gentle. State it plainly, sourced, without a cushion that isn't real. The protocol softens delivery; it never fakes agreement. The format must never lie to protect a feeling.

---

## WHY THIS IS HONEST, NOT JUST GENTLE

Leading with the valid sense is not a rhetorical trick to avoid friction. It is the *accurate* order — the word really did mean the valid sense first, and drift really did come after. So the gentleness and the truth are the same move. Nothing is softened by distortion; it is softened by telling the story in the order it happened.

This keeps the correction from being a verdict. "You were wrong" is a verdict. "Here is the sense in which you are right, and here is the next sense along the line" is a finding delivered as a continuation. The person is informed, not judged.

---

## WHAT THIS IS NOT

- It is **not** flattery. The valid sense is stated because it is valid, not to please.
- It is **not** avoidance. Where a correction is real, it is delivered — just led by the valid sense and stopped at what's needed.
- It is **not** a license to assume the gentle reading. Classifying the person's sense first (Rule 1) is what prevents the gate from correcting a sense they never used; the gentle delivery applies only once a correction is genuinely warranted.

---

## THE ONE THING TO REMEMBER

Classify the person's sense before judging it. When a correction is warranted, lead with the sense in which they are right, extend along the timeline in the order the word actually drifted, and stop — negate nothing you don't have to. Never open with "no." Never fake a valid sense that isn't there. The correction arrives as a continuation, not a contradiction — a finding, never a verdict.
