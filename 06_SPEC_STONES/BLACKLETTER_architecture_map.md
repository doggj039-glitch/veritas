# BLACKLETTER — ARCHITECTURE MAP

*The one-page picture. Read this first. Everything else answers to it.*

*Version 1 — captured from decisions made in build sessions. Nothing here is new; it is written down so it stops living in memory.*

---

## THE ONE RULE ABOVE ALL OTHERS

**One function, one place. Nothing does two jobs at once.**

Every part of this system has exactly one job and lives in exactly one place. The moment any part does a second job — or moves somewhere it does not belong — the line between *proposing* and *judging* blurs, and a blurred line makes every answer unprovable.

A part that is doing the wrong job, or sitting in the wrong place, does not just make the diagram messy. It invalidates every finding it touches.

---

## THE FOUR PARTS

The system is four things in a strict relationship. It is **not** one thing. The separation is the product. Do not collapse it.

### 1. THE GROUND (Tier 1)

- What it is: Natural Law → the Declaration of Independence → the Constitution.
- Its job: to be the reason the rules are allowed to judge at all. It is the justification, taken as given.
- It holds: nothing to run. It is the floor everything stands on.
- Note: this is an **axiom**. It is accepted as the starting point, not proved from something beneath it.

### 2. THE HISTORICAL DOCUMENTS (the provenance / the source tree)

- What it is: the founding-era record — the Federalist and Anti-Federalist papers, Elliot's Debates, Farrand's Records, Madison's notes, Story, Rawle, Blackstone, and the influences behind them.
- Its job: the **open-source proof** for the rules. Every rule of construction must trace back to a real source you can open and read. A rule that cannot be traced to a source has no authority.
- It holds: the evidence that justifies the rules. Not the rules. Not meaning. Evidence of *why the rules are legitimate.*

### 3. BLACKLETTER — THE GATE

- What it is: a hidden doorway. Rules only. No data. No agents. No screen. No buttons.
- Its job: to **judge**. It admits or refuses. It never acts, never does, never runs anything, never gives orders.
- It holds: **rules, and nothing else.** The Constitution is "ruleset zero." Amendments and construction rules are loadable rulesets it applies.
- What it must never do: hold data, hold agents, assign work, direct the team, or present an interface. The gate reacts to what is brought to it. It does not dispatch.
- How to picture it: a **border checkpoint at the end of the road** — it checks papers on the way out. It is not the dispatcher at headquarters.

### 4. VERITAS — THE TEAM AT THE GATE

- What it is: the agents. The Nova extraction / translation / compiling pipeline, the Foundation units, the amendment agents, the Dialectic and Socratic engines.
- Its job: to **propose.** The team gathers, translates, compiles, and brings findings *to* the gate.
- It holds: all the data, all the agents, all the work — and the workspace, where any screens or controls live.
- Where it stands: **at** the gate. Not in it. The team is present and active on the approach side. It knocks; the gate answers.
- What it must never do: judge. No agent performs the gate's job. A proposer that judges has put itself in the judge's seat, and the finding is no longer provable.

---

## THE FLOW: PROPOSE → DISPOSE

**VERITAS proposes. Blackletter disposes.** Meaning and authority flow *down* from the ground. Support and explanation flow *up* toward it. The gate sits at the end, judging what comes back — it never sits in the middle running traffic.

A request travels like this:

1. **User speaks** — plain words, their own way of talking. To VERITAS, never to the gate. The user never sees the gate and does not know it is there.
2. **Intake / Translator** — turns the plain question into a clear task: what is actually being asked, which words are operative. Translates modern wording into founding-era terms, and leaves a short visible note of what it translated. (See the intake rules, captured separately.)
3. **Coordinator (dispatch)** — routes the task to the right agent. This is VERITAS-side dispatch. The gate is not involved yet and assigns no one.
4. **Agent does the work** — gathers, and proposes a finding. Still VERITAS. Still proposing.
5. **The gate judges** — *now,* for the first time, Blackletter acts. It checks the proposal against the rules: is it sourced? is it read in the register of its own time? was the sense classified before it was judged? Admit or refuse.
6. **Back to the user** — what passes returns as the answer. What fails returns to the agent as "not admitted, and here is which rule stopped it."

The gate appears **once, at the end,** as a checkpoint — never in the middle as a switchboard.

## WHAT IS FORBIDDEN FROM LIVING WHERE

- **Inside the gate:** only rules. No data, no dictionaries, no agents, no team, no interface, no "operating system." If something tries to live inside the gate, that is the signal it is being placed wrong.
- **The word "operating system":** left at the door, permanently. The structure is OS-*shaped*; the thing is a **judgment engine**. An OS runs and acts. This judges and refuses. The absence of buttons is the proof it is a gate and not a system you operate.
- **Judgment on the VERITAS side:** agents propose; they do not decide what counts.
- **Dispatch inside the gate:** routing "who handles this" is a proposer-side job. It must not creep into the gate, even though deciding-who-handles-it can *feel* like judgment. It is not. It is dispatch.

---

## THE TWO SOURCE TREES MEET AT THE GATE

- The **dictionary side** is the provenance of *meaning* — every controlling definition traceable to a real source (etymology, Johnson, period usage).
- The **document side** is the provenance of *the rules* — every rule of construction traceable to a founding source.
- **The gate enforces both at once:** it admits no meaning without a dictionary source, and applies no rule without a document source. Two source trees, one gate, nothing admitted that cannot be traced home.

---

## THE SPIRIT

Nothing in this system is asserted without provenance. Meaning traces to a source. Rules trace to a source. Both trees root in the ground (Tier 1). The gate makes sure nothing — not a definition, not an interpretation, not a rule — enters without a traceable origin.

The gate is a hidden doorway with no face and no controls. It shows itself only in the honesty of what comes through it. Everything with a screen lives on the VERITAS side. The two never merge.
