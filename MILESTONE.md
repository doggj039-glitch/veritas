### What was completed at this milestone
- **The Gate (Blackletter)** — resolves any word to its verbatim Johnson 1773 meaning, or
  throws it out as *Void for Vagueness*. Etymology, founding-era usage, and the Supreme Court
  cases that construed each term — with their dissents. 10/10 regression tests green.
- **Ten grounded voices** — Federalist, Anti-Federalist, Ratification, Blackstone, Montesquieu,
  Story, the Federal Convention, Rawle, Lieber, Sedgwick. Verbatim, cited, no invention.
- **The library, readable** — the founding books open and scroll in-app; all 7,696 Supreme
  Court cases are searchable; the drift model, the analysis layer, and the Socratic walkthroughs.
- **The bridge** — a local engine that makes the app a real tool, not a gallery. Fully offline.
- **The Ask-Claude tier** — optional, retrieval-augmented, gate-fenced; the key stays on the
  machine; the model composes only from grounded sources.
- **The pull engine** — API-free downloads. Give it a source; it fetches, parses, and files.
  First pulls: Locke's *Second Treatise*, Cruden's *Concordance* (1785).
- **Hybrid window** — a clean app that hands external links to the system browser.

### What it is, and why it exists
VERITAS treats the Constitution the way it was written to be read: as fixed operative terms —
binary, not "living." The gate admits the founding meaning and voids the drift. Where modern
doctrine stretched a word, the record shows exactly when and by whom. It is a checkpoint, not a
verdict: it grounds meaning so the argument can be made honestly.

### How it was built
Entirely through words. Not one line of this was written by the person whose vision it is —
who, by their own account, never got past `hello world` in fifty years, and called coding their
nemesis. They didn't learn to code. They made the machine understand what they meant. The thesis
that drove it — *that the Constitution is binary and only works that way, and that modern law is
the software glitching out society* — is now an instrument you can run.

The first program wasn't `hello world`. It was this.
